Exercise for Joint Health: What Works Best

By Dr. David Lee, Orthopedic Surgeon August 22, 2025

When your joints are stiff and sore, exercise might be the last thing on your mind. It's a common myth that exercising with joint pain, such as from arthritis, will only make it worse. However, the opposite is true: the right kind of movement is one of the most effective ways to manage joint pain, improve flexibility, and maintain your independence. The key is to choose exercises that support your joints rather than stress them.

1. Low-Impact Aerobic Exercise

Low-impact cardio gets your heart rate up without putting heavy stress on your joints. These activities help improve circulation, control weight, and boost your mood. The best options are those that provide smooth, continuous movement.

  • Swimming and Water Aerobics: Water supports your body weight, taking nearly all the pressure off your joints while providing gentle resistance.
  • Cycling: Whether on a stationary bike or outdoors, cycling is a great way to strengthen the leg muscles that support your knees.
  • Walking: A simple, accessible, and effective way to keep your joints moving. Start with short distances and gradually increase your pace and duration.

2. Strength Training

Building strong muscles is like creating a natural brace for your joints. The stronger the muscles surrounding a joint, the less stress the joint itself has to endure. You don't need to lift heavy weights to see benefits.

  • Resistance Bands: These are inexpensive, versatile, and perfect for providing gentle resistance to build muscle.
  • Bodyweight Exercises: Modified squats, wall push-ups, and bridges use your own body weight to build strength effectively.
  • Light Weights: Using small dumbbells for controlled movements can significantly improve muscle tone and joint stability.

3. Flexibility and Range of Motion

Stiffness is a major complaint for those with joint issues. Exercises that focus on flexibility help reduce stiffness, improve your range of motion, and decrease your risk of injury. The goal is to gently move your joints through their full, comfortable range.

  • Daily Stretching: Gentle, slow stretching of major muscle groups should be a part of your daily routine, especially after warming up.
  • Yoga: Look for gentle or restorative yoga classes that focus on slow movements, stretching, and proper alignment.
  • Tai Chi: This ancient practice involves slow, flowing movements that improve balance, flexibility, and strength without impacting joints.

Move with Confidence

Before starting any new exercise program, it's important to talk to a professional. Our orthopedic specialists and physical therapists can help you design a safe and effective plan tailored to your specific needs.
